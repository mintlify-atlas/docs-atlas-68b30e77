Pedal offers a range of vehicles to suit every trip — from city commutes to regional road trips across Peru. This page explains the rental categories available in the booking form and the body-type classifications used to describe each vehicle's shape and style.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Rental categories">
    These are the four car types you can select when filling out the booking form on Pedal.

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Compacto (Compact)" icon="car">
        Small, fuel-efficient vehicles ideal for navigating city streets and tight parking. Best suited for solo travelers or couples exploring Lima or other urban centers. Easy to maneuver in high-traffic areas like Miraflores and San Isidro.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Sedán" icon="car-side">
        Classic four-door cars with a separate trunk. Comfortable for small families or business travelers who need a balance of cabin space and fuel economy. A popular choice for intercity travel between Lima and Ica or Arequipa.
      </Card>
      <Card title="SUV" icon="truck-suv">
        Sport utility vehicles with elevated ground clearance and more passenger or cargo space. Well suited for mixed terrain, regional travel, or groups that need extra room. Good option for routes through Ancash or Piura.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Camioneta (Pickup)" icon="truck">
        Pickup trucks with an open cargo bed. Ideal for carrying equipment, luggage, or supplies on longer trips. A practical choice for travelers heading into more rugged or rural areas.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Tab>

  <Tab title="Body types">
    These are the vehicle body-type classifications listed in the Pedal site footer. They describe the physical shape and design of a car, independent of its rental category.

    <CardGroup cols={2}>
      <Card title="Sedán" icon="car-side">
        A three-box design with separate compartments for the engine, passenger cabin, and trunk. Offers a quiet, enclosed ride with good boot space. Common examples in the Pedal fleet include the Honda Accord, Chevrolet Cruze, and Volvo S60.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Coupé" icon="car">
        A two-door body style with a sloping roofline and sportier profile. Typically seats two to four passengers. Coupés prioritize style and driving dynamics over practicality.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Hatchback" icon="car">
        A compact body style with a rear door that opens upward to access a combined passenger and cargo area. More versatile cargo space than a sedán in a smaller footprint. Good for city driving.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Descapotable (Convertible)" icon="sun">
        A car with a retractable or removable roof for open-air driving. Suited for scenic coastal or highland routes when weather permits.
      </Card>
      <Card title="Familiar / Station Wagon" icon="car-rear">
        An extended body style with a full-height cargo area behind the rear seats. Maximizes luggage capacity while maintaining full seating for five or more passengers. Ideal for family trips or long-distance travel with heavy baggage.
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Vehicle characteristics comparison

The table below summarizes key attributes across body types to help you choose the right vehicle for your trip.

| Body type | Typical seats | Cargo space | Best use case | Fuel type in fleet |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sedán | 5 | Medium | City travel, business trips, intercity | Petrol, Diesel |
| Coupé | 2–4 | Small | Short trips, style-focused driving | — |
| Hatchback | 4–5 | Flexible | Urban commuting, compact travel | Petrol |
| Descapotable | 2–4 | Small | Leisure, scenic routes | — |
| Familiar / Station Wagon | 5–7 | Large | Family travel, long-distance trips | Petrol, Diesel |
| SUV | 5–7 | Large | Mixed terrain, regional travel | Petrol, Diesel |
| Pickup (Camioneta) | 4–5 | Open bed | Cargo transport, rural routes | Petrol, Diesel |

## Fuel types

Most vehicles in the Pedal fleet run on petrol. The following models use diesel:

- **Honda CR-V** — Automatic, 14 Km/lt
- **Chevrolet Trailblazer** — Automatic, 10 Km/lt
- **Volvo XC90** — Automatic, 9 Km/lt
- **Mitsubishi Pajero** — Automatic, 10 Km/lt
- **Volkswagen Passat** — Automatic, 14 Km/lt

<Note>
  Diesel vehicles are all automatic and generally offer larger cabins (5–7 seats). They are well suited for longer regional trips where fuel efficiency and carrying capacity matter.
</Note>
