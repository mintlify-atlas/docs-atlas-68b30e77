## Navigation bar

The Pedal website features a fixed navigation bar that remains visible at the top of the page as you scroll. It has a white background and contains the **PEDAL** brand logo on the left, a set of anchor links to each page section, and a **Únete** (Join) button on the right.

The navbar is fully bilingual. Each link and button carries `data-en` and `data-es` attributes so the interface language can switch between English and Spanish without a page reload.

### Nav links

| Label | Anchor | Section contents |
|---|---|---|
| Inicio | `#home` | Hero section with the site slogan and a car booking form |
| Cómo funciona | `#howDoesItWork` | Three-step rental process walkthrough |
| Alquiler | `#topCars` | Vehicle catalog with brand filter buttons |
| ¿Por qué elegirnos? | `#service` | Key reasons to choose Pedal |
| Contáctanos | `#contact` | Newsletter subscription form and contact details |

### The "Únete" button

The **Únete** (Join) button sits at the right end of the navbar. It carries `data-en="Join"` and `data-es="Únete"` for internationalization, consistent with the rest of the nav links.

## Page sections

The page is made up of eight distinct sections rendered in sequence.

### Hero — `#home`

The opening section displays the brand slogan:

> Libertad sobre ruedas a tu disposición.

It also contains a booking form where visitors can begin the rental process directly from the landing view.

### How it works — `#howDoesItWork`

A visual walkthrough of the three steps required to rent a car with Pedal. Each step shows an icon, a title, and a short description. See [How it works](/guide/how-it-works) for full details.

### Vehicle catalog — `#topCars`

A filterable grid of available rental vehicles. Brand filter buttons let visitors narrow down the catalog by manufacturer. Clicking a filter toggles the visibility of matching vehicle cards via JavaScript — no page reload is needed.

### Peru map section

An embedded Google Maps view showing Pedal's coverage across Peru. A department modal opens when the user clicks a region, giving location-specific information. The modal closes on a second click or when the user clicks outside it.

### Why choose us — `#service`

A feature highlight section listing the advantages of renting through Pedal. Each item presents a benefit with a supporting description.

### User reviews

Three customer testimonials displayed as cards. This section has no dedicated anchor link in the navbar but sits between `#service` and `#contact` in the page flow.

### Contact — `#contact`

A newsletter subscription form paired with contact details. Visitors can enter their email address and accept the terms to subscribe. See [Contact & newsletter](/guide/contact) for full details.

### Footer

Contains a brand logos strip above the footer row. The footer itself lists quick links (Preguntas frecuentes, Términos y condiciones, Reportar, Documentación, Política de la empresa, Privacidad), contact details, and social media icons.

## Active link highlighting

The navbar automatically highlights the link that corresponds to the section currently in the viewport. As the user scrolls, a JavaScript scroll listener checks which section anchor is closest to the top of the screen and applies an active style to the matching nav link. No user interaction is required — the highlight updates continuously as the page is scrolled.

## Loading screen

When the page first loads, an animated loading screen is displayed over the entire viewport. The loader plays a short car animation video (`car_loader.mp4`) while the page assets initialize.

<Info>
  The loader tracks how many `<img>` elements on the page have finished loading. Once every image has fired its `load` event, the loading screen fades out and the main content becomes interactive.
</Info>

This ensures visitors never see a partially rendered page with broken or missing images.
